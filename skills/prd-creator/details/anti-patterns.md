# PRD Creation  -  Anti-Patterns

## Anti-Pattern 1: Vague Goals
DON'T: "Improve user experience."
DO: "Reduce onboarding drop-off from 35 percent to 20 percent within 30 days."
Why: Vague goals are not measurable or actionable.

## Anti-Pattern 2: Missing Non-Goals
DON'T: Leave scope boundaries implicit.
DO: Explicitly state what is out of scope.
Why: Non-goals prevent scope creep and clarify intent.

## Anti-Pattern 3: Requirements as Implementation Details
DON'T: "Build a React table with infinite scroll."
DO: "Support browsing 10,000 records with filtering and pagination."
Why: Requirements should describe outcomes, not solutions.

## Anti-Pattern 4: No Rollout Strategy
DON'T: Assume a big-bang release with no plan.
DO: Choose a rollout method and define mitigation steps.
Why: Rollout plans reduce risk and allow controlled learning.

## Anti-Pattern 5: Missing Success Metrics
DON'T: Ship without a measurement plan.
DO: Define metrics and how they will be tracked.
Why: Without metrics, impact is unclear.

## Anti-Pattern 6: Missing Test or Production Plan
DON'T: Skip test strategy or production requirements.
DO: Define test coverage and rollout/rollback requirements.
Why: Without a test and production plan, delivery risk is higher.
